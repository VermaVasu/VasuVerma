Intro

Hi! I’m Vasu Verma and I am passionately curious. I hail from India; a land not only rich in its history & tradition but also in its pool of scientists and engineers.

My enthusiasm for the unlimited scope of electronics began, ever since I made my first Solar Powered RC Bot in High School and watched all the small components come together with such intricacy.

My undergraduate degree is a Bachelor of Technology in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the National Institute of Technology, Jaipur. Electronics engineering is an intricate, multidisciplinary never-ending puzzle that I am passionately engaged in solving. I believe that with the right combination of programming and circuitry tinkering we can improve the lives of people around the world. So, now, in industry, this passion remains with me. There's certainly yet more to learn, yet more problems to solve, and yet more to build. And for this, I am very grateful.

Projects

Debug HSSI between DLP controller and DMD

Worked on debugging of Texas Instruments Proprietary HSSI signals flowing between DLP(Digital Light Processing) Controller and DMD(Digital Micromirror Device). Analyze and plot Clock/Data Eye Diagram without any external probing or oscilloscopes.

ARDUINO BASED LI-FI PROTOTYPE

A college funded project on ways to improve the speed of data transmission using modulation schemes on MATLAB and indigenously developed Li-Fi based transceivers.

Exploring biomarkers for early detection of COVID

Data analysis on Patient’s Sp02 measurements found using Pulse Oximetry. Studied it’s direct relation for detecting early onset of COVID Pneumonia to assist in time quarantining and hospitalization of at risk individuals.

BOLT IOT AND ML PROJECTS

Worked on Bolt IoT platform based several IoT projects based on c++, python and APIs, polyfit and Z-Score ,some of which have helped me have a good grip on knowing about integration of several devices and also implementing ML .

Optimization of 2 stage operational amplifier

Designed a Two stage single ended output Differential Amplifier (differential + Gain stage) with power dissipation less than 200nW. The main features achieved through the design were Slew Rate greater than 10V/uS and Gain Bandwidth = 5MHz using LTSpice tools
